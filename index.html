Who this is for

This pilot is for pastors, chaplains, ministry workers, and others in Christian pastoral care who feel a quiet spiritual or ministry burden - not only burnout on paper, but the weight of carrying others while rarely having a safe place to receive themselves.

By "spiritual burden," we mean the quiet weight you may carry in ministry, prayer, pastoral care, or personal spiritual life. For many, it may feel like persistent heaviness, emotional exhaustion, trouble resting, a sense of being constantly on alert, sudden waves of fear, numbness, or memories and pressures that keep returning after caring for others. This is not a medical or psychological diagnosis.

You do not need to perform spiritually here. You do not need to report a dramatic experience.

What this is

A short, Christian receiving experience in a private setting.

You may be invited to watch or listen to a brief receiving video, remain quiet for a few minutes, and then note - on a simple scale - how your sense of spiritual burden, inner rest, and ministry strain feels before and after.

We are in an early learning stage. Honest feedback matters more than impressive stories.

What this is not

  • Not medical treatment or psychotherapy.
  • Not a replacement for your church, doctor, counselor, or other appropriate care.
  • Not a paid program or a request for public testimony.
  • Not a promise of healing, visions, signs, or any guaranteed result.

Full covenant

Free Pastor Receiving is a small, private, pastor-first receiving experience for pastors, chaplains, and ministry workers who are carrying quiet spiritual or ministry burden.

It is free. It is voluntary. It does not require a public testimony.

The purpose is simple: to create a quiet space to receive, reflect, and honestly record what changes, if anything, before and after the experience.

This is a Christian receiving experience centered on quietness, prayerful openness, and careful reflection.

You may be invited to watch or listen to a short Christian receiving video, remain still for a few minutes, and then complete a simple before/after reflection about spiritual burden, inner rest, and ministry strain.

You are free to stop at any time.

This is not medical treatment. This is not psychotherapy. This is not a replacement for your church, pastor, doctor, counselor, or other appropriate care.

This is not a request for public testimony. This is not a paid program. This is not a promise of healing, visions, signs, or any guaranteed result.

By participating, you agree that:

  • You are choosing to participate voluntarily.
  • You may stop at any time, for any reason.
  • You will not be pressured to share your name, church, or public testimony.
  • You will not treat this as medical or psychological care.
  • If fear, confusion, distress, compulsion, or a sense of losing touch with reality increases, you will stop and seek appropriate support.
  • If you are currently under medical, psychiatric, counseling, or pastoral care, you will not stop that care because of this experience.
  • Your feedback may be used internally to improve the process. Any public use of feedback must be anonymized and separately approved.

You may use your real name or an anonymous participant name.

We will not publicly share your name, church, location, health condition, or private feedback without your explicit permission.

Early feedback is used mainly to understand whether this experience feels clear, safe, voluntary, and meaningful for pastors.

FAQ

Do I have to share my church name or appear publicly?

No. You may use an alias. We do not ask for public testimony.

Is this therapy or treatment for depression or anxiety?

No. This is not medical or psychological treatment. If you have clinical concerns, please continue appropriate professional care.

What if I feel worse or uncomfortable during the experience?

Stop. You may end at any time. If fear, confusion, or distress increases, do not push through. Seek appropriate support.
